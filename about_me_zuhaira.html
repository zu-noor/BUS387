About Me

I am a Computer Science student at Washington and Lee University with a passion for technology, art, and building inclusive communities. As an international student, I've embraced the opportunity to connect diverse perspectives and serve as a bridge between different cultures and experiences.

My academic journey in Computer Science has equipped me with problem-solving skills and technical knowledge, while my artistic pursuits allow me to express creativity and maintain balance. I thrive in collaborative environments where I can apply my technical abilities while nurturing meaningful connections with those around me.

Previous Projects

As an International Student Mentor at Washington and Lee University, I've played a key role in helping international students navigate their transition to campus life in the United States. Our mentorship program supports approximately 60 international students each year, providing guidance on everything from academic resources to cultural adjustments.

I've contributed to several technology-focused projects during my time at Washington and Lee, applying my computer science knowledge to create practical solutions. My academic work has allowed me to explore various programming languages and development methodologies.

Through my artistic endeavors showcased on my Instagram (@zn._arts), I've developed a portfolio that reflects my creative vision and technical skills. My artwork serves as both a personal outlet and a way to connect with others who share similar interests.

My View of the World

I believe that my close friends and family are my most valuable assets. These relationships have shaped who I am and continue to inspire my personal and professional growth. Having experienced life across different cultural contexts, I've come to value the richness that diverse perspectives bring to any community.

"The connections we build become the foundation for understanding and bridging differences in our increasingly interconnected world."

As an international student and mentor, I've witnessed how genuine human connections can transcend cultural and linguistic barriers. This experience has reinforced my belief that empathy and open communication are essential for creating inclusive spaces where everyone can thrive.

I approach both my technical work and community involvement with a commitment to accessibility and understanding. My goal is to use my computer science skills alongside my interpersonal abilities to create technology and communities that bring people together rather than divide them.
